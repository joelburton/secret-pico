Wiring»

In case a wire has become loose, here’s how things are connected:

  • Red wires carry power; black wires connect to ground. The entire blue rail at each end is connected, so everything connecting to that is grounded. Same for the red rail and power.
  • The Pico provides power/ground to the rails: ground from pin #3, and to power rail from pin #5.
  • The button is connected to ground at pin #13 and to pin #15 (which we use to determine if the button is pressed or not)
  • The speaker (a “piezo”, the round black circle) is connected to the yellow wire that goes to pin #20 (which is where signal to generate sound are sent), and to ground on blue rail.
  • The LED (white square on blue board) gets power and ground from the rails, and gets a signal via the green wire from pin #19. (This LED is a type that can be chained together to make a long series of LEDs; the brand for it is a NeoPixel).
  • The screen is connected to ground and power from the rails, and gets the signal of what to display (“SDA”) from yellow wire to pin #21, and the clock timing (“SCL”) from blue wire to pin #22.
  • The blue knob (a “potentiometer”) is connected to power/ground from the rails, and is read from green wire to pin #31.

Danger

Don’t create a short circuit!

Do not accidentally connect a red wire to the blue ground rail, or a black wire to the red power rail; this can result in a short circuit and potentially damage things!
